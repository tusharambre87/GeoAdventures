## What to Do First When You Arrive

Take the lift directly to Level 2 first — not the summit. Level 2 has the best sightlines and a glass floor panel kids find thrilling. Once you've had 20 minutes on Level 2, decide if the summit is worth it (another queue, but the view changes are marginal).

---

## Insider Tips Most Guides Don't Tell You

- Book the 'Summit access' ticket — it includes Level 2. Do not book the 'Level 2 only' ticket and then decide you want the summit; you can't upgrade on the day.
- The tower is most photogenic from the Trocadéro esplanade across the Seine. Walk there first for the classic photo before entering.
- The lifts have two queues: ticket holders and stair climbers. The stair queue moves faster if your kids are old enough (steps to Level 2 only).
- Wednesday and Sunday evenings have a light show at 10pm (visible from outside, free). Not a reason to change your day but worth knowing.

## Where to Eat Nearby

These are the best-reviewed restaurants within reach of Eiffel Tower — ranked by Google rating and family suitability.

### Les Cocottes — Tour Eiffel
**4.3 ⭐ Google** | Modern French bistro | 5 min walk

Christian Constant's casual bistro — excellent quality at reasonable Paris prices. Kids' portions available. The casserole-style dishes are served in cast iron pots, which kids find fun.

### Café de Flore
**4.2 ⭐ Google** | Classic Parisian café | 20 min walk (Saint-Germain)

One of Paris's most famous cafés. The croque-monsieur and chocolat chaud are the moves. Moderately expensive but the experience is genuinely iconic. Busy at midday — arrive before 12:15pm.

### Marché Saxe-Breteuil (market)
**4.6 ⭐ Google** | Open air market | 10 min walk

Wednesday and Saturday mornings only. One of Paris's most beautiful markets, directly in the shadow of the tower. Cheese, baguette, fruit, and rotisserie chicken — perfect picnic supplies for the Champ de Mars.

## What to Do Next — Nearby Experiences

Eiffel Tower pairs naturally with these nearby experiences. Each one makes a full half-day or full day:

### Bateaux Mouches River Cruise *(River cruise)*

4.3 ⭐ Google, 10,000+ reviews. Departs from Pont de l'Alma, 7-minute walk from the tower. The 70-minute cruise passes under 20 bridges and gives the best views of Haussmann Paris. Kids can move around freely on the open deck. Runs every 30 minutes. Best in late afternoon.

### Champ de Mars Picnic *(Outdoor space)*

The park directly below the tower is one of the best picnic spots in the world. Bring supplies from Marché Saxe-Breteuil (Wed/Sat) or any nearby boulangerie. Lawn is open, flat, and families spread out here naturally. Children run freely and the tower overhead never stops being impressive.

### Trocadéro Carousel *(Activity)*

The ornate carousel at the Trocadéro esplanade (directly across the Seine from the tower) costs €3 and has been running since the 1930s. Classic Paris experience for young kids, and the Eiffel Tower in the background makes for the best photo of the trip.

## Logistics

Metro Line 6 to Bir-Hakeim (exit under the tower) or RER C to Champ de Mars. No car access — drop-off only on avenue Gustave Eiffel. Fully accessible with lifts to all levels. Stroller storage available at the base. Baby changing on Level 1. Bag search on entry adds 10–15 minutes to arrival time.
