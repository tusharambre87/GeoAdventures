## Is London Good for Families?

London is an outstanding family destination when approached correctly. Our Kid-Fit Index scores London **93/100 for ages 5–8** and **89/100 for ages 9–12**. Even families with toddlers do well here — 80/100 for under 5s.

London is very stroller-friendly. The Tube requires planning (not all stations have lifts) — use the TfL app to find accessible routes. Buses are easier with small kids.

## The Top 6 Stops — Ranked by Kid-Fit Score

### 1. Science Museum — 94/100

**Best for:** 5+ | **Best time:** Morning | **Duration:** 2 hrs

The free floors are better for kids than the paid exhibitions.

### 2. Natural History Museum — 93/100

**Best for:** 4+ | **Best time:** Morning | **Duration:** 2.5 hrs

Go straight to the dinosaur gallery on arrival — it gets crowded fast.

![The iconic Natural History Museum, London — head straight to the dinosaur gallery](/images/blog/london-family-guide-2026/natural-history-museum.jpg)

### 3. Hyde Park Playground — 88/100

**Best for:** 2–10 | **Best time:** Afternoon | **Duration:** 90 min

The Diana Memorial Playground is genuinely world-class. Under 12s only.

![Kids playing in Hyde Park — one of London's best free family afternoons](/images/blog/london-family-guide-2026/hyde-park.jpg)

### 4. Tower of London — 85/100

**Best for:** 7+ | **Best time:** Morning | **Duration:** 2 hrs

The Crown Jewels queue moves fast. Start there before it builds.

![The Tower of London — a family favourite with history that actually engages kids](/images/blog/london-family-guide-2026/tower-of-london.jpg)

### 5. Borough Market — 84/100

**Best for:** All ages | **Best time:** Lunch | **Duration:** 60 min

Thursday–Saturday only. Best lunch stop in central London.

### 6. London Eye — 82/100

**Best for:** 5+ | **Best time:** Late afternoon | **Duration:** 60 min

Book the last session of the day for sunset views. Much less crowded.

![The London Eye at sunset over the River Thames](/images/blog/london-family-guide-2026/london-eye.jpg)

## What to Skip (Honest)

These are the stops that look good on travel blogs but consistently underperform for families.

### Madame Tussauds
Expensive, crowded, and kids lose interest after 40 minutes. Not worth the price for families.

### Buckingham Palace
You can't go inside. Standing outside looking at gates for 20 minutes is not a family activity.
