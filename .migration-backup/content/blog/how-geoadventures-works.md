When you enter a destination into GeoAdventures, something interesting happens behind the scenes. This isn't a generic list of tourist attractions — it's a plan built specifically for your family.

## Step 1: Understanding your family

Before we suggest a single stop, we look at who's coming. Are there toddlers who need nap windows? Older kids obsessed with history? A baby who needs a stroller-friendly route? Every family is different, and the plan should reflect that.

## Step 2: Selecting the right stops

Our AI evaluates hundreds of potential stops against a family fitness score. We're not just asking "is this a good place?" — we're asking "is this a good place *for this family, on this day, in this weather, with these kids*?"

Stops are scored on:
- **Kid engagement** — will children aged 3–10 be interested?
- **Practicality** — are there restrooms? Is it stroller-friendly? Does it require booking?
- **Energy pacing** — is this a high-energy stop that should come early, or a wind-down spot for the afternoon?

## Step 3: Sequencing for real family life

A great itinerary isn't just a list of places — it's a story. We sequence stops to minimise backtracking, front-load the highest-energy experiences, build in food stops at sensible times, and always include at least one "reset" location (a park or playground) where kids can run freely.

## Step 4: The AI intelligence layer

Each stop in your plan gets a snapshot: the best time to visit, what to do first when you arrive, whether queues are likely, and how long to realistically spend there with children. This is the data that turns a good stop into a confident decision.

## Step 5: Live support on the day

Plans change. Kids get tired. It rains. GeoAdventures travel mode gives you a rescue button — instantly suggesting food stops, easier alternatives, or a break — whenever you need it.

The result is a plan that feels less like a guidebook and more like a co-pilot who's been on this trip before.
