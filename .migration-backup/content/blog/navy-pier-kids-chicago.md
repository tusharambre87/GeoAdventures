## What to Do First When You Arrive

Head directly to the Centennial Wheel when you arrive — queues build fast on weekends. After that, walk east along the pier toward the Pier Park rides. The Children's Museum is best saved for mid-visit when younger kids need a calm reset.

---

## Insider Tips Most Guides Don't Tell You

- The south side of the pier has significantly fewer crowds and the same lake views as the main promenade — use it to decompress.
- Centennial Wheel tickets are sold at the base — buy them the moment you arrive, not later when queues double.
- Navy Pier is dramatically less crowded on Tuesday–Thursday mornings, even in summer. Weekend afternoons are the worst time to visit.
- The fireworks on Wednesday and Saturday evenings (June–August) are free and genuinely spectacular from the pier itself.

## What to Do Next — Nearby Experiences

Navy Pier pairs naturally with these nearby experiences. Each one makes a full half-day or full day:

### Shoreline Water Taxi *(Boat cruise)*

The water taxi between Navy Pier and Michigan Avenue Riverwalk runs every 15 minutes (April–October). $8 adults, $5 kids. One of the best-value ways to see Chicago's lakefront from the water. Much less formal than an architecture tour — kids can move around freely.

### Chicago Children's Museum *(Indoor museum)*

Inside Navy Pier itself (4.4 ⭐ Google, 1,200+ reviews). Best for ages 3–10. Plan 60–90 minutes. The 'Tinkering Lab' and 'WaterWays' exhibits are consistently the most popular with families. Buy tickets online to skip the box office queue.

### Bike the Lakefront Trail *(Outdoor activity)*

Bobby's Bike Hike (4.8 ⭐ Google, 2,000+ reviews) rents family bikes and surreys right next to Navy Pier. The lakefront path is completely flat, runs 18 miles, and has no car traffic. A 45-minute casual ride north to Oak Street Beach and back is perfect with kids.

## Logistics

Navy Pier has a free indoor parking garage (validate at attractions inside). Stroller-friendly throughout. Accessible restrooms at both ends of the pier. The CTA bus route 29 (State Street) drops directly at the entrance.
