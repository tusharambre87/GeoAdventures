Travelling with children is one of life's great adventures — but it can also feel overwhelming without a plan. After working with thousands of families, we've gathered the tips that actually make a difference.

## 1. Build in buffer time

Kids operate on their own schedule. Allow an extra 30 minutes at every transition: hotel check-outs, airport security, boarding trains. Buffer time is never wasted — it becomes the unexpected ice cream stop or the moment your 6-year-old spots a street cat.

## 2. Let them carry their own bag

Even a 3-year-old can carry a small backpack with their favourite toy, a snack, and a colouring book. Ownership of their bag = investment in the trip.

## 3. Front-load the hard stuff

Queuing for big attractions? Do it on day one when energy is highest. Save the relaxed parks and beaches for day three when everyone needs to recharge.

## 4. Build in a "nothing day"

For every 3 days of sightseeing, plan one half-day with zero agenda. Let the kids lead. Some of our families' best travel memories come from an unplanned morning at a neighbourhood playground they would have driven past.

## 5. Feed before you go

Never visit an attraction on an empty stomach. Always have snacks in the bag, and eat a proper meal before any museum or long walk.

## 6. Give kids a mission

Before each stop, give your child one thing to find or look for. "Find the oldest thing in this room" or "spot three animals before we leave." Missions turn passive sightseeing into active exploration.

## 7. Download maps offline

International data can be expensive and unreliable. Always download your destination city's offline map before you travel.

## 8. Have a rainy day plan

Check the weather on day one and identify one indoor option for each day. If it's sunny, you don't need it. If it rains, you're the hero.

## 9. Keep evenings light

Children need downtime to process the day. Avoid booking evening activities on consecutive days. A slow dinner near the hotel beats rushing to a 7pm show with overtired kids.

## 10. Talk about the trip before you go

Spend 10 minutes looking at photos or videos of your destination together before you travel. Kids who know what to expect are calmer and more engaged when they arrive.
