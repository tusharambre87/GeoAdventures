There's a tension every travelling parent knows: you want to capture the moment, but reaching for your phone means you're half-watching it instead of living it.

Here are some practical ways to get the shots that matter — and put the phone away for everything else.

## Choose 3 moments a day, not 300

Before you set out each morning, decide on three moments you *want* to photograph. The arrival at a new city. Your child's face at a specific attraction. Dinner together. When you know your three shots, you're present for everything else.

## Let kids take photos

Hand your child the phone for 10 minutes at each stop. The photos are often terrible. They're also often the ones you treasure most — the blurry shot of a pigeon, the close-up of their sibling's ear. It also keeps them engaged.

## The "don't smile" rule

Posed photos capture what you looked like. Candid photos capture what you *felt*. Try putting the camera away until someone does something — then shoot. The resulting photos are almost always better.

## Video one minute a day

A single 60-second video — your child describing what they did today, filmed on the hotel bed at the end of the day — becomes irreplaceable. Their accent, their vocabulary, what they cared about at this age. Film it.

## Write three words each evening

Not a diary — just three words that describe the day. "Rainy. Croissants. Laughing." Three words is achievable. Three words becomes a poem when you read it back years later.

The goal isn't to document everything. It's to come home with a few things that make you feel it all over again.
